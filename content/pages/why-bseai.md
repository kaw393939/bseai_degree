## A degree for the people who will ship AI inside real organizations

We are not building another generic AI degree. We are building a degree for
the people who will design, implement, govern, and improve AI-enabled systems
inside real organizations.

## The one-sentence claim

The B.S. in Enterprise AI prepares students to design, deploy, evaluate, and
operate enterprise-grade agentic systems at the intersection of software,
data, workflows, and business value.

## An Enterprise-Implementation Degree, Not A Generic AI Major

Most organizations are not blocked because they lack access to a model. They
are blocked because they do not have enough people who can turn model
capability into reliable systems.

BSEAI is built around the real implementation bottlenecks:

- deployment
- integration
- workflow design
- evaluation
- governance
- technical communication

That is the labor market where institutional value is actually being created.
See [Enterprise Deployment Gap](/enterprise-deployment-gap) for the underlying
stats, and [Role Clusters and AI Work](/role-clusters-and-ai-work) for the
jobs this degree targets.

## The Peer-Program Landscape (Why Our Positioning Is Distinct)

The peer set is becoming more crowded. Multiple institutions in the region
and beyond now offer undergraduate AI degrees, but most are centered on
computer science, mathematics, algorithms, and traditional AI subfields.
BSEAI does not try to out-CS the CS-heavy degrees. It claims the territory
of enterprise implementation, agentic workflow design, data integration, UX,
systems analysis, productization, and responsible deployment.

The full seven-program comparison table is on
[Peer Programs and Positioning](/peer-programs-and-positioning).

## Why NJIT Can Own This Space

NJIT is not starting from zero. The program inherits teaching habits, course
infrastructure, and applied orientation from the Information Systems tradition
the author has been building since 2002 (see [Author Context](/author-context)).

Those strengths are already visible in the current curriculum:

- integration across systems
- project-based learning
- applied technical delivery
- close connection to employability and real-world outcomes

BSEAI should be presented as the focused Fall 2026 launch of that capability,
not as nostalgia for a retired degree. NJIT's advantage is that this program
emerges from a practical, deployment-oriented Information Systems tradition
rather than from a purely theoretical or research-first model.

## What The Studio Spine Produces

The target is an early-career builder who can work across the studio spine:

- IS 117 and IS 218 — web and application development (Product Engineering)
- IS 265 — information systems and stakeholder framing
- IS 331 — data and database systems
- IS 390 — requirements analysis and systems design
- IS 392 — AI-driven text analytics
- IS 465 — data analytics for business information systems
- IS 480 — data-centric AI
- IS 425 — enterprise AI applications and infrastructure
- IS 491 / IT 491 — senior project / capstone

Every major claim in the presentation should be traceable to a course, a
studio habit, a project artifact, or a capability that can be reviewed. That
is what makes the degree easier to defend in a room.
