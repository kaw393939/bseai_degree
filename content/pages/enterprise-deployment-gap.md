## The shortage is not researchers. It is implementers.

The shortage is not only in AI researchers. The shortage is in professionals
who can understand what AI can do, integrate it into enterprise systems,
evaluate reliability and risk, and turn it into real-world workflows and
value.

## The Gap, In Numbers

Adoption is broad. Production scale is rare. These two lines must be read
together — they are the defining shape of the enterprise AI market in 2025–26.

**McKinsey — State of AI 2025.**

- **88%** of organizations report using AI in at least one business function.
- About **one-third** of firms have begun to scale AI programs.
- **39%** report enterprise-level EBIT impact.
- **23%** are scaling an agentic system somewhere in the organization.
- **≤10%** are scaling agents in any single function.

**S&P Global — 2025 Voice of the Enterprise.**

- **42%** of firms report abandoning most AI initiatives before production
  (up from **17%** the prior year).
- **~46%** of projects are scrapped between proof-of-concept and broad
  adoption.

**BCG — AI at Work 2025.**

- **13%** of employees see AI agents deeply integrated into their daily work.

**NY Fed — AI at Work 2026.**

- **~18%** of firms had adopted AI by year-end 2025.
- **~78%** of the US labor force works at firms that have adopted AI.

Read together: adoption is broad, but institutional depth is thin. This is the
labor-market asymmetry BSEAI is built to answer.

## Where Projects Actually Stall

The deployment gap is not mysterious. Projects usually stall in familiar
places:

- data quality and retrieval design
- integration with existing systems
- workflow redesign and human review
- evaluation and failure analysis
- governance, documentation, and monitoring
- stakeholder trust and change management

These are not side issues. They are the real operating surface of enterprise
AI — and they map directly onto the BSEAI studio spine. See
[From Freshman To Professional](/from-freshman-to-professional) for the
course-to-role mapping.

## Why Employers Care

The market rewards people who can:

- connect models to interfaces and workflows
- translate enterprise needs into system behavior
- evaluate reliability and risk
- move a pilot into production
- instrument a system so it can be reviewed, governed, and improved

That is a different target from frontier-model research, and a much better
target for a broad undergraduate degree. See
[Role Clusters and AI Work](/role-clusters-and-ai-work).

## The Curricular Response

If the bottleneck is implementation, then the curriculum should be built
around implementation work. Students should graduate ready to operate in the
layer where organizations are actually struggling: integration, evaluation,
workflow redesign, governance, and deployment.
