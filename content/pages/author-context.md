## Twenty-three years at NJIT, one through-line

For over two decades, I've been teaching at New Jersey Institute of Technology
(NJIT), working with students from Newark and the broader region. My role has
evolved from adjunct instructor to building the Enterprise AI program, but the
core mission has remained constant: helping students from all backgrounds
build careers in technology.

## The Long Run — NJIT 2002 to 2026

- Adjunct instructor since 2002.
- Founded the **Web Systems (WIS)** program at NJIT.
- Created **8** regularly offered undergraduate and graduate courses.
- Taught **more than 10,000** students across **15 sections annually**.
- Built curricula that Fortune 100 companies recruited from.

Evolving the WIS program into the Enterprise AI degree — a natural progression
driven by my research work and industry projects using Generative AI to train,
augment, and automate software engineering and product development processes.

## The Research

When ChatGPT launched, I started using it within days — and immediately
recognized that everything had changed. Since then, I've immersed myself in
studying LLM and vector embedding technology, building a series of research
projects through NJIT capstone courses that implement advanced ETL pipelines
combining LLM and machine learning techniques for NLP and knowledge graph
construction to support RAG workflows for personalized knowledge product
production.

As an early adopter of Vibe coding, I've combined my deep knowledge of
enterprise software engineering and product development processes to develop
innovative uses of Generative AI in enterprise software development — including
AI-augmented QA review and advanced techniques to leverage AI in the
day-to-day workflows of software engineers.

## The Classroom

This degree argument is grounded in sustained contact with students, not only
with research literature.

- Newark and the broader NY/NJ region, 23 years.
- AmeriCorps at an urban after-school youth center in Pittsburgh.
- Software engineering instruction for government employees in Zambia,
  implementing the e-government system and introducing UX design practices
  using Axure prototyping.

This history is why the curriculum argument on this site places so much weight
on whole-student formation: judgment, communication, governance, and
apprenticeship, not just tool fluency. See
[Student Formation and the Studio Gap](/student-formation-and-the-studio-gap).

## Origin Story

My technology journey started early — my father was a mainframe systems lead
operator for Alcoa, and I grew up in a data center surrounded by various
computers. In 1983, I received a Tandy TRS-80 Color Computer 2 for Christmas
and taught myself to program.
