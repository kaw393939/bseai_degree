## Applied roles, not frontier-research fantasies

Job titles will change. What remains more stable is the pattern of work
underneath them. That pattern is what matters for curriculum design.

Frontier model R&D is concentrated in a small number of labs. Deployment work
is broad. BSEAI is built for the broad side — these are applied roles, not
frontier-research roles.

## Cluster 1: AI Product Engineering

Turns model capability into interfaces, tools, and workflows that people can
actually use.

- product and interface design
- workflow shaping
- application delivery
- user-facing system behavior
- iteration based on feedback, evals, and measured failure modes

This is where the degree's web, application, UX, and systems courses become
directly legible.

## Cluster 2: Forward-Deployed And Integration Work

Sits at the boundary between organizations and technical systems.

- understanding enterprise context
- translating workflow needs into system design
- moving prototypes toward production
- supporting adoption and change management
- making systems useful in the real environment they must live inside

This is where Information Systems logic remains a major advantage.

## Cluster 3: Deployment, Evaluation, And AI Operations

Owns the reliability layer.

- retrieval and data access patterns
- evaluation and failure analysis
- monitoring and instrumentation
- infrastructure and enterprise controls
- governed rollout under operational constraints
- runtime evidence and release discipline

See [Managing Drift and Runtime Integrity](/managing-drift-and-runtime-integrity).

## Why These Clusters Fit The Curriculum

Students are not being prepared for one narrow title. They are being prepared
for adjacent families of work that share the same core abilities:

- systems thinking
- workflow design
- data fluency
- evaluation discipline
- communication with stakeholders
- responsible deployment judgment

## What Employers Actually Need

The enterprise problem is no longer model access alone. The problem is
finding people who can turn AI into:

- repeatable workflows
- governed systems
- measurable outcomes
- products users can trust

That is why these role clusters are a better target than a generic "AI major"
story.
